Iteration by iteration

comparison between optimization and musical performance

Steps in the procedure of HS are as follows:

Step 1. Initialize a Harmony Memory (HM).

Step 2. Improvise a new harmony from HM.

Step 3. If the new harmony is better than minimum harmony in HM, include the new harmony in HM, and exclude the minimum harmony from HM.

Step 4. If stopping criteria are not satisfied, go to step 2.

-.Random init
-.Sort by aesthetic estimation
-.Choose from HM
-.Same chance: 33.333%
-.Exchange worst with improvised if it's better than of worst
-.Keep doing until satisfied

Consider this simple example

- Initialize - Improvise (1,2,3) - Exchange (5,3,3) - Iterate until (2,3,1)

HMCR

Harmony memory consideration rate

Rules? Randomness?
Random number, 0-1
Above HMCR: random selection (Possible values, Notes)
HMCR: 0.95 : 95% HM

PAR

[1, 2, 4, {6}, 7, 9]

Escape local optimal?
Random number, 0-1
Above PAR: Do not ajdust
PAR: 0.1 : 10% Adjust

An Example

Possible values of an instrument (a variable) is:
{C, D, E, F, G}

HMCR is 0.95, PAR is 0.10
The instrument now has: {C, E, G} in HM.

95% From C,E,G
5% From {C, D, E, F, G}
When {E} is chosen: E Can be shifted to {D} or {F} with 10% probability.
